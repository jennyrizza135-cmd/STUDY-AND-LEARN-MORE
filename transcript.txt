      <h2>Practical Tips for Effective Studying</h2>
      <ol class="tips-list">
        <li>
          <strong>Set clear goals for each study session.</strong><br />
          Before you start, write what you want to achieve, for example:
          “Finish chapter 3 and answer all practice questions.” Clear goals keep you focused.
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Use active learning instead of just reading.</strong><br />
          Summarize in your own words, teach the topic to an imaginary friend, or create mind maps.
          Active learning helps your brain connect and store information better.
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Study in short, focused blocks (Pomodoro technique).</strong><br />
          Work for 25 minutes, then rest for 5 minutes. After 4 rounds, take a longer break.
          This reduces fatigue and keeps your concentration high.
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Test yourself regularly.</strong><br />
          Use flashcards, quizzes, or practice questions. Self‑testing shows what you really know and
          what you still need to practice.
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Space out your study sessions.</strong><br />
          Instead of cramming in one night, review material several times over days or weeks.
          This “spaced repetition” makes memories stronger.
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Create a distraction‑free environment.</strong><br />
          Put your phone away, close unnecessary tabs, and tell people you are busy for a while.
          A quiet, organized space helps your brain focus.
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Take care of your body.</strong><br />
          Sleep, healthy food, water, and movement all affect how well you can think, focus, and remember.
        </li>
      </ol>
